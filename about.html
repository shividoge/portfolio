About

Builder first.

I’m Shivin — a robotics leader and researcher who likes turning messy real-world constraints into systems that are clear, reliable, and actually run.

My through-line

Whether it’s robotics or research, I’m obsessed with the same question: how do you make a system that works without constant babysitting? I like projects where sensing, control, and design decisions all matter — and where the final result is something a team can operate and improve.

I’m currently at Middleton High School’s Engineering Magnet (Tampa, FL), and I lead Middleton Robotics while serving as a Head Researcher at the University of South Florida.

Principles I build by

  • Clarity beats cleverness. If a teammate can’t debug it, it’s not done.
  • Make signals visible. Telemetry and logs prevent “guess engineering.”
  • Optimize handoffs. Integration is where most projects fail.
  • Document like a teammate. Notes are leverage — not overhead.
  • Teach to multiply. The goal is others building confidently, not me doing everything.
